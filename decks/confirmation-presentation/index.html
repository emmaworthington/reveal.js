Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation

(AMOC)

[www.rapid.ac.uk]

  • Upper layers of warm, salty water moving northwards to seas around Greenland
  • Water cools, becoming dense enough to sink
  • This and Antarctic Ocean are only regions of deep water formation
  • Cold, dense water returns equatorwards at depth
  • Very large amounts of heat moved northward
  • Heat lost to atmosphere - moderates climate
  • Slowing of AMOC - more heat recirculates in gyre
    • affects intensity of hurricanes
    • affects rainfall in Sahel due to ITCZ position
  • Moorings are nearly full height
  • Moorings close to edges of basin to estimate geostrophic flow
  • Measurements taken every 15 minutes but post-processing are 12-hourly
  • Schematic shows upper and lower NADW more clearly

Assumptions of linear regression models

  • Linearity
  • No multi-colinearity
  • Homoscedasticity (equal variance of residuals)
  • No autocorrelation of residuals
  • Normal distribution of residuals

Four-variable OLS regression model failed 3 assumptions as it is based on a time series

  • Linear relationship between each explanatory variable and response variable
  • Variability explained by one explanatory variable can't be explained by another, i.e., removing one should make sufficient difference
  • Is the variance of the residuals dependent on the explanatory variables?
  • Is a residual dependent on the previous value?

Conclusions

  • Using a generalized least-squares with autocorrelated errors model based on RAPID monthly mean density anomalies explains 73% of the variance in the observed transport
  • Omitting the WB2 failure period of late 2005 to early 2006 may give better results.
